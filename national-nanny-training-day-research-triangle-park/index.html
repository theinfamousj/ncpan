National Nanny Training Day: Research Triangle Park

| ABOUT | REGISTER | PREVIOUS | PRESENT | SPONSOR |

National Nanny Training Day is held on the Saturday at the end of the Week of the Young Child, and is a day of back-to-back workshops designed to improve childcare by providing schedule-friendly, affordable, professional development/continuing education to those who raise and care for children. It was created by Lora Brawley of NannyBizReviews. Sue Downey of Nannypalooza controls the brand and licenses nanny agencies and nanny support organizations (hey, that’s us!) to produce a local event. There are over 40 local event productions all happening simultaneously on the same day, providing education to over 2,200 child care givers.

Our local event is tailored to the needs of our attendees, and will be done at a central location to all points of the triangle. We have attendees that range from full time, employed nannies to parents who want to improve their parenting skills, with all levels of child care providers in between. We usually have four one-hour workshops, each to certify attendees in a different skill. In the past, they have been: car seat safety (installation and child placement) from UNC Hospitals, children’s activity and movement from Be Active Kids, speech and language development, and the duties of the nanny as a mandatory reporter of child abuse and child neglect.

Be a Presenter at the Next National Nanny Training Day: Research Triangle Park

Do you have something to teach our attendees? Use the form below to express interest. Speakers will be considered on a first-come-first-serve basis.

  • Presenters must be have prior experience presenting to an all-adult audience.
  • Workshops will be 1 hour in length, including a question-and-answer period. Shorter workshops are considered.
  • Workshops can be on any topics relevant to nannies.

As compensation, presenters are given a free pass to the event to use as they wish, specialized advertising in the event program, and a token thank you basket as budget allows.
